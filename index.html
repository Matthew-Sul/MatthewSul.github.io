Overview

Project involves morphing faces with each other, particularly myself and Richard Horvitz in this case and getting the mean of a population of faces, Danes tho thought i was gonna have a lotta doggos to morph (which would’ve been cool)

Part 1: Defining Correspondences

Used the correspondences tool in the spec to mark myself and Richard in a fairly close manner. After loading the images and the points, got their average and used it to compute the delaunay triangulation of both of them to use in future parts.

Part 2: Computing the “Midway Face” (my arch nemesis, this project)

The crucial step to computing the midway face of the aforementioned images in addition to any other images i want to morph in future steps. First compute the average shape/keypoint location averages in the 2 faces. Get the target points (to be compared to the original points from images and use fraction of 1/2 (for warp frac and color dissolve) since want the middle of the images. Afterwards I morph both faces I have into that shape by looping through all of the triangles calculated from Delaunay triangulation in the previous step. Needed an affine transformation matrix between the two triangles for the project. Used draw.Polygon to get the pixels within the triangle so can grab the colors from original image and put it to the midway image. After inverse warping the pixels, and going through all the triangles, color dissolving the colors together, should be presented with the midway image.

Part 3: (Meep) Morph Sequence

Morph is pretty similar to Midway image, however instead of just one midway image, we want to create 45 frames to use and animate the morph between myself and Richard. Midway image had a set fraction of 1/2 but for the morph, want to have fractions evenly spaced between 0 and 1 (0 would be purely im1 and 1 would be purely im2). Did a morph of each fraction and then assembled all the frames together to create a gif of the morph.

Part 4: The “Mean (non-angy) Face” of a Population

Used the Dane’s dataset with 200 of the neutral faces (asked OH and said it fine) and got their corresponding…. correspondences (heh) keypoints associated with each image. None of them had image corner points so manually added corner points to each image (and by extension the keypoints for images). Process very similar to midpoint images but this time had target points be the average of all the points in each dane image. Morphed every image in set and their points comparing it to average points, adding them all up, aftewards just averaging the images to get the average image. Reused the correspondence tool to get points of the average points and a resized image of myself. Ran the code for midway images to get a midway image of myself and the Danes Avg img.

Part 5: Caricatures Extrapolating From the Mean

Midway image used a fraction of 0.5 to be the average of 2 images, Morph had fractions ranging from 0 to 1, so tried to use fractions outside the range 0-1 to get caricatures and see the results )i.e. -0.5 and 2)
